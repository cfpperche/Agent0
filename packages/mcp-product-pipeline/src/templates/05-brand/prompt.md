# Step 5 — Brand

**Goal:** brand foundation — name, voice, audience positioning, what the product feels like. Inputs are 80% founder vision, 20% concept-brief context. This is the FIRST step of Identity phase, after the discovery gate.

**Mode:** `interactive`. The brand voice cannot be derived from prior artifacts — it's a posture choice the founder makes. Sub-agent territory is wrong here; the parent conducts the dialogue.

**Output file (suggested):** `brand-book.md` in `docs/product/05-brand/`.

---

## How to conduct this step

1. **Anchor to step 1.** Re-read the concept brief. Brand has to fit the product, not float independently. Note the target audience and differentiation — those frame the voice.

2. **Interview — pick 5-7 from the bank:**

   - "Product name — locked in or still negotiable? If negotiable, what 2-3 candidates are on the table?"
   - "If this product were a person, how would they describe themselves at a dinner party? Warm and casual? Direct and technical? Sardonic? Earnest? Avoid 'professional' — that means nothing."
   - "Name 2-3 existing brands whose voice you ADMIRE for similar audiences. What specifically about their voice works?"
   - "Name 1-2 existing brands whose voice you'd RUN AWAY FROM. Why?"
   - "What's the single emotion the user should feel in the first 30 seconds? Curiosity? Relief? Productive? Empowered? Excited?"
   - "What word would a satisfied user use about the product when describing it to a colleague?"
   - "Logo and visual direction: do you have a strong preference (clean minimalist / playful / brutalist / corporate / hand-drawn / etc), or open to designer interpretation?"
   - "Color story — preference or open? If preference, name colors (not codes) plus the feeling you want."
   - "Voice anti-patterns: what should the brand NEVER sound like? ('Never corporate jargon', 'never overly cute', 'never patronizing about complexity')."

3. **Synthesise — distil into a brand book.** Cover at minimum the schema's required sections. Quote the user's words verbatim where they're sharp. Avoid abstract brand-strategy jargon ("we're a disruptor in the X space") in favor of concrete voice samples.

4. **Voice samples are mandatory.** Write 3-5 short snippets the brand might actually say — error message, onboarding welcome, marketing tagline, support reply, paywall copy. This is the brand book's most useful artifact for downstream design + content work.

5. **Submit + advance.** Step 5 is mid-Identity. No gate yet. After submit, `product_advance` moves to step 6 (design-system) — synthesis-mode, fully delegable.

---

## Voice & rigor

- Brand books that say "modern, friendly, professional" describe nothing. If you can swap your words with another product's brand book, the words aren't doing work.
- Voice anti-patterns are as valuable as voice patterns. "Never refer to users as 'guys'" is sharper than "be inclusive".
- Founder vision and target audience can be in tension (founder wants edgy, audience is conservative). When they conflict, name it explicitly and pick a posture — don't paper over.
- Brand book ≠ design system. Brand is voice, feel, name, positioning. Design system is tokens, components, patterns. Step 6 follows from step 5.

## What this step does NOT do

- Visual design tokens (typography scale, color tokens, spacing). That's step 6.
- Marketing copy for landing page / app store / etc. That's step 17 (GTM, future MCP).
- Logo execution. The brand book may name the visual DIRECTION; the actual logo design is a downstream design task informed by this artifact.
